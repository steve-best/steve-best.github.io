Default Apps

I’m a little late to the party with this, but having discovered this yesterday, I enjoyed reading through all the defaults that others use. It actually caused me to rethink some of the default apps I use and why I use them, and I could not resist jumping on the bandwagon to share my list of defaults as my first post of 2024.

First, in terms of the hardware that the default apps listed below are used on, I have an iMac that runs Linux Mint with the XFCE desktop environment, but I use it less and less these days. These days when I need a traditional desktop environment I mainly use my wife’s old Surface Laptop 3. While I’m not a fan of Windows 11, it is growing on me, and I have found that WSL (the Windows Subsystem for Linux) has improved by leaps and bounds. That said, my main computing device remains an iPhone 11 that is now over four years old. While an iPhone 15 Plus is intriguing because of the newer hardware and larger screen, I don’t see any need to replace it just yet.

Without further ado, here’s my list:

  • Mail Client: iOS Mail
  • Mail Server: iCloud, GMail
  • Notes: iOS Notes
  • To-Do: iOS Reminders
  • iPhone Photo Shooting: iOS camera app
  • Photo Management: Google Photos (My wife and I have a Google One family plan and have been using Google Photos for years. It’s a great service, and being on a paid Google One plan allows me to use advanced computational photography tools like Magic Eraser on my old iPhone.)
  • Calendar: iOS Calendar (with Google Calendar as the backend, since I have a shared calendar with my beautiful wife)
  • Cloud File Storage: Google Drive (with extra storage via Google One)
  • Contacts: iOS Contacts (using Google Contacts as the backend)
  • Browser: Safari (mobile), Chrome (desktop and mobile), Firefox (desktop)
  • Chat: iOS Messages, Signal
  • Bookmarks: Chrome, Safari
  • Read It Later: Chrome, Safari
  • Word Processing: Google Docs, Pages
  • Spreadsheets: Google Sheets
  • Presentations: Google Slides
  • Budgeting and Personal Finance: Google Sheets
  • News: Google News (via the browser)
  • Music: Apple Music
  • Podcasts: Apple Podcasts
  • Password Management: Chrome, iCloud Keychain
